About QR Codes

QR codes (Quick Response codes) are two-dimensional barcodes that can store a variety of information, such as URLs, text, contact details, and more. They can be scanned using a smartphone camera or a dedicated QR code reader app.

Common Uses of QR Codes:

  • Marketing and Advertising: Businesses use QR codes in advertisements, posters, and flyers to direct customers to their websites or promotional content.
  • Product Packaging: QR codes on product packaging provide additional information about the product, such as usage instructions or nutritional facts.
  • Event Management: Event organizers use QR codes for ticketing and check-ins, allowing attendees to quickly access event details.
  • Contactless Payments: QR codes facilitate secure and convenient payments through mobile payment apps.
  • Wi-Fi Access: QR codes can store Wi-Fi credentials, allowing users to connect to a network by simply scanning the code.

With the increasing adoption of smartphones, QR codes have become a popular tool for sharing information quickly and efficiently. They offer a seamless way to bridge the physical and digital worlds.
